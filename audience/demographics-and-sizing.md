# Target Audience Demographics & Market Sizing
*Research compiled: 2026-04-08*
*Sources: 30+ web searches, peer-reviewed studies, industry reports*

---

## Executive Summary

The core audience for a chronotype-aware behavior enforcement app is **18-35 year olds, slightly female-skewed (55-60%), concentrated in the US, India, Japan, UK, and Germany**. The problem (social jet lag, bedtime procrastination, morning wake-up difficulty) affects 69-70% of the working/studying population but is most acute in Gen Z and Millennials. This demographic also has the highest app subscription spending and the strongest response to gamification.

## 7. Occupational Patterns

### Which professions/lifestyles have the worst social jet lag?

| Occupation | SJL Severity | Key Data | Source |
|-----------|-------------|----------|--------|
| **University students** | **WORST** | 65% have >1h SJL; 50% have >2h; avg 88.3 min; chronotype peaks evening at age 19-21 | DovePress; SLEEP (Oxford) |
| **Shift workers** | **WORST** | Most affected population overall; early shifts devastate evening types, night shifts devastate morning types | Juda/Vetter/Roenneberg 2013 |
| **Healthcare workers (early shifts)** | Very high | Nursing students: only 18% are morning-type but forced into early rotations | PMC chronotype studies |
| **Office workers (inflexible 9-5)** | High | Inflexible work schedule is a predictor of higher SJL | PMC social jet lag review |
| **Remote workers** | Lower (potentially) | Flexible schedules COULD reduce SJL — but many adopt worse habits (later bedtimes, doom scrolling) | Nature 2025; theoretical |
| **Retirees** | **Lowest** | Employed individuals have more SJL than retirees; SJL drops sharply after 60 | PMC; Australian study |

### Factors that predict worse social jet lag

| Factor | Effect | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Younger age | More SJL | Consistent across all studies |
| Evening chronotype | Much more SJL | Roenneberg; multiple |
| Inflexible work schedule | More SJL | PMC review |
| Smoking | Associated with higher SJL | PMC review |
| Soft drink consumption | Associated with higher SJL | PMC review |
| Living in urban area | Slightly more SJL | Australian National Sleep Health Foundation |
| Higher education level | Slightly more SJL | Nature 2023 nationally representative survey |
| Male sex | Very slightly more SJL (but women have more bedtime procrastination) | Mixed data |

### The paradox of remote work
- In theory, flexible schedules should reduce social jet lag
- In practice, remote workers often adopt LATER chronotypes and MORE bedtime procrastination
- The loss of external structure (commute, office arrival) removes zeitgebers
- **This means remote workers are a valid target — they have the freedom to fix it but can't**

## 8. Composite Target Audience Profile

### Primary target (80% of marketing)
- **Age:** 18-32
- **Gender:** 55% female, 45% male
- **Geography:** US (#1), India (#2 by volume), UK (#3), Japan (#4), Germany (#5)
- **Occupation:** Students, young professionals, early-career workers with inflexible schedules
- **Income:** Middle income ($30-80K US or equivalent)
- **Device:** Cross-platform; iOS for US/Japan, Android for India/Germany/Brazil
- **Subscription spending:** $15-50/month on app subscriptions; $61/month avg on health/fitness
- **Chronotype:** Evening or intermediate type (82% of 18-30 population)
- **Behavioral profile:**
  - Doom scrolls 4+ nights/week past intended bedtime
  - Sets 2+ alarms, hits snooze 2-3 times
  - Has tried 3-5 solutions (default alarm, Alarmy, alarm across room, sleep sounds)
  - Knows what to do but can't execute
  - Responds to challenges > gentle encouragement
  - Active on TikTok and Reddit

### Secondary target (15% of marketing)
- **Age:** 28-40
- **Gender:** 50/50
- **Occupation:** Tech workers, product managers, entrepreneurs
- **Income:** $80-150K US or equivalent
- **Behavioral profile:**
  - Already has a routine, wants to optimize it
  - Quantified self enthusiast
  - Will pay annual subscription immediately
  - Shares scores on LinkedIn
  - Highest LTV customer

### Tertiary target (5% of marketing)
- **Age:** 16-20
- **Gender:** 55% male, 45% female (gaming-adjacent)
- **Occupation:** High school / university students
- **Income:** Limited; parents may pay
- **Behavioral profile:**
  - Highest viral coefficient (1 student = 5 downloads)
  - Gamification-native
  - Lowest ARPU but highest social sharing
  - Japan/Korea over-indexed
