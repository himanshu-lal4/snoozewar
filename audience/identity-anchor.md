## What Are We?

A **chronotype-aware sleep-wake cycle enforcement system.**

Not an alarm app. Not a sleep tracker. Not a habit coach. We are an **enforcer with a personality** that owns the **entire loop** — from the moment you should be winding down at night to the first 30 minutes after you wake up.

Because here's what every alarm app gets wrong: **you can't fix the morning without fixing the night.** If you went to bed 2 hours late, no alarm app on earth is making you feel good at 6:30 AM. The morning is already lost by midnight.

We are the thing that:
- At **night**: enforces your bedtime before you doom-scroll into 1 AM
- At **wake**: forces you out of bed with missions you can't cheat
- In the **first 30 minutes**: owns the decision void where every good intention dies

Your 11 PM self makes promises. Your 6:30 AM self breaks them. We don't let either version of you off the hook.

## What Problem Are We Solving?

### The Surface Problem
"I can't wake up at my intended time" — but it starts the night before.
- 57% of adults hit snooze daily
- 82% who try to become morning people quit within 21 days
- Average person has tried 3-5 times to fix their mornings and failed every time
- **But most of them went to bed 1-2 hours late the night before** — revenge bedtime procrastination, doom scrolling, "just 5 more minutes" that turns into 90 minutes
- If you don't sleep enough, no alarm, no mission, no app can make you feel awake. The morning failure is a night failure in disguise.

### The Real Problem
**Social jet lag** — a mismatch between your biological clock and your social schedule.

69-70% of people live with more than 1 hour of social jet lag. They're not lazy. They're not undisciplined. They're fighting their own biology — and losing. Their chronotype (which is ~50% genetic, governed by 351 gene variants) says "wake at 8 AM" but their job, their culture, their Instagram feed says "real success starts at 5 AM."

The result: guilt, shame, failed routines, and a daily loop of "I'll do better tomorrow."

### The Deeper Problem
**Two decision voids, not one.**

**The night void:** At 11 PM, your self-control is at its lowest (end-of-day depletion). You tell yourself "just 5 more minutes" on your phone. 90 minutes later, you've stolen sleep from tomorrow's version of you. This is revenge bedtime procrastination — the only "free time" people feel they have. **If we don't intervene here, the morning is already ruined.**

**The morning void:** Sleep inertia lasts 15-70 minutes after waking. Your prefrontal cortex is functionally offline. Every "good intention" you had the night before — the workout, the journal, the healthy breakfast — dies here. Not because you lack willpower (the willpower model is debunked), but because your brain literally cannot execute complex decisions. **And it's 10x worse if you only slept 5 hours because nobody enforced your bedtime.**

These two voids feed each other in a vicious cycle:
```
Late night → Short sleep → Harder wake-up → Worse morning →
Guilt all day → "I deserve my evening" → Late night again
```

No app breaks this cycle because no app owns both sides:
- **Alarm apps** (Alarmy) force you awake, then abandon you. Zero night-side enforcement.
- **Habit apps** (Fabulous, Streaks) assume you're already awake and motivated
- **Wellness apps** (Calm, Headspace) track and suggest, never enforce
- **Sleep trackers** (Oura, RISE) detect your chronotype but never intervene
- **Screen time apps** (Opal, one sec) limit phone use but don't connect it to your wake time

**We own the full cycle: Night → Wake → Morning → Day.** That's not a feature list — it's the entire thesis. Fix one side without the other and you've fixed nothing.

They're not looking for another app that tells them to "take a deep breath" or "set a bedtime reminder." They want something that **actually forces them** to follow through on both sides — and maybe roasts them a little for trying to cheat.

## What Is Our Motive?

### What we believe:
1. **Your chronotype is not a flaw.** Being a night owl isn't laziness — it's genetics. We adapt to your biology, not the other way around.
2. **Willpower is a myth.** The willpower/ego-depletion model was debunked across 36 labs and 3,531 participants. Stop telling people to "just try harder."
3. **Enforcement works when encouragement fails.** Alarmy proves this with $15M+/year and 82M downloads. But enforcement without science is just annoying. Enforcement WITH science is transformative.
4. **The "5 AM Club" is marketing, not science.** We don't care what time you wake up. We care that you wake at YOUR time — and that your time is aligned with your biology.
5. **Consistent timing + adequate sleep = the whole equation.** Waking at the same time every day is critical — but not if you're running on 5 hours because nobody enforced your bedtime. We care about both: the right amount of sleep AND the right timing. One without the other doesn't work.

### What we refuse to do:
- Shame people for being late chronotypes
- Promise to turn night owls into 5 AM risers (max shift: 1.8-2.4 hours)
- Claim phone screens are light therapy (110 lux vs 2,500+ needed)
- Use the debunked "21-day habit" myth (real average: 66 days)
- Pretend snoozing once is catastrophic (Sundelin 2024 says it's not)
- Exploit users — we enforce because they asked us to, not to extract attention

Six things are converging right now that make this the moment:

1. **AlarmKit (iOS 26, announced June 2025)** — For the first time, Apple is letting third-party apps create system-level alarms. Before this, iOS alarm apps were crippled. The #1 technical barrier to building a serious alarm app on iOS just disappeared.

2. **FDA wellness guidance (January 2026)** — New guidelines derisked behavioral sleep apps from medical device classification. We can talk about chronotypes, sleep consistency, and circadian alignment without triggering FDA oversight — as long as we frame it as behavioral wellness, not clinical treatment.

3. **"Sleepmaxxing" trend (100M+ TikTok posts)** — Gen Z and millennials are actively obsessed with optimizing sleep. The cultural awareness is already there. We don't need to educate the market on why sleep matters — we need to give them a tool that actually enforces it.

4. **Social jet lag is entering mainstream vocabulary** — Academic concept from Roenneberg that's now appearing in mainstream health media. Nobody owns it as a brand position yet. First-mover window: 12-18 months before someone else claims it.

5. **Post-pandemic schedule chaos** — Remote/hybrid work shattered fixed schedules. More people than ever have flexible wake times but no structure to hold them accountable. The problem has gotten worse, not better.

6. **Subscription fatigue is pushing quality over quantity** — Users are cutting subscriptions. The ones that survive are daily-use, can't-live-without apps. We're building for that bar from day 1.

**The window is open. It won't stay open forever.**
