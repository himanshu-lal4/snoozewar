- Coined in **2006** by Till Roenneberg (Ludwig-Maximilian University, Munich)

## 4. The "Wrong Timezone" Framing

### Roenneberg's Own Metaphor — He Already Uses It

This is gold: **Roenneberg himself uses the timezone metaphor** in his scientific communication.

From his work and *The Marginalian* coverage:
> "While all people from Chicago physically live in Chicago, their body clocks virtually 'live' east or west of Chicago depending on their chronotype, with every chronotype virtually living in its own chronobiological time zone that — unfortunately in many cases — is different from where the individual actually lives."

He maps it as:
- **Larks** "live" slightly east of their city (ahead of social time)
- **Owls** "live" slightly west (behind social time)
- **Intermediate types** "live" near the city center

### Is "Living in the Wrong Timezone" Established?
- **YES** — Roenneberg uses it as his primary explanatory metaphor
- The 2006 paper describes social jet lag as "being between two time zones: that of your body clock and that of society's clock"
- The metaphor is scientifically grounded, not just marketing spin

### Why This Framing Works for Marketing
1. **Instantly understandable** — everyone knows what jet lag feels like
2. **De-shames the user** — "You're not lazy, you're jet-lagged by society"
3. **Creates an identity** — "I'm a -2 hour person" or "My body lives in a different timezone"
4. **Implies a solution** — if you're in the wrong timezone, the fix is alignment
5. **Novel but not weird** — builds on a concept everyone already understands

### Competing Metaphors/Framings
| Framing | Used By | Strength |
|---------|---------|----------|
| "Sleep debt" | RISE | Quantifiable, guilt-inducing |
| "Sleep score" | Oura, Sleep Cycle | Gamified, but abstract |
| "Chronotype" | Various | Scientific, but jargon-heavy |
| **"Wrong timezone"** | **Us (from Roenneberg)** | **Instantly relatable, emotionally resonant, novel** |
| "Sleep hygiene" | Generic health | Boring, preachy |
| "Circadian rhythm" | Academic | Too technical for mainstream |

**Our framing: "Wrong timezone" > "sleep debt" > "chronotype" for mainstream marketing.** It's the most human, most relatable, most shareable.

## 7. Fear-Based vs. Aspiration-Based Marketing

### What the Research Says

**The academic consensus is nuanced:**

#### Fear Appeals (Loss Framing)
- Fear-based ads get **higher click-through rates** and conversions short-term
- Fear appeals require **4 conditions** to work: high perceived susceptibility, high perceived severity, high response efficacy, high self-efficacy
- **These 4 conditions are almost never all fulfilled** — making most fear appeals ineffective
- Fear can cause **avoidance behavior** — people tune out rather than act
- In diabetes context: patients recognized scare tactics, resulting in "increased feelings of anxiety, incompetence and negativity"
- Loss-framed messages increase **anxiety** with "little-to-no concomitant benefits" (84-country COVID study)

#### Aspiration Appeals (Gain Framing)
- Gain-framed messages are more effective for **prevention behaviors** (which our app is)
- Loss-framed messages are more effective for **screening/detection behaviors** (doctor visits)
- Gain-framed messages evoke **positive affect**, loss-framed evoke **negative affect**
- The Truth anti-smoking campaign switched from scare imagery to positive collective action and saw **better results**
- For **health-affirming behaviors** (daily routines, habit building), aspiration beats fear

#### The Hybrid Approach (RECOMMENDED)

The most effective health messaging combines both:
1. **HOOK with fear** (grab attention): "2 hours of social jet lag doubles your diabetes risk"
2. **RESOLVE with aspiration** (drive action): "Find your perfect wake time in 7 days"

### How This Maps to Our Marketing

**Phase 1 — Awareness (Fear/Shock hooks):**
- "69% of people wake up in the wrong timezone"
- "Your alarm clock is fighting your DNA"
- "2 hours of social jet lag doubles your diabetes risk"
- "You're not lazy. You're chronically jet-lagged."

**Phase 2 — Solution (Aspiration framing):**
- "Find YOUR perfect wake time"
- "Wake up when your body actually wants to"
- "What if your alarm worked WITH your biology?"
- "Align your life to your chronotype"

**Phase 3 — Retention (Identity framing):**
- "I'm a Wolf chronotype — that's why I thrive at night"
- "My social jet lag went from 3 hours to 30 minutes"
- "Life Score: 87. Best month ever."
