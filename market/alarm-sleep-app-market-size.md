**Estimated alarm app-only TAM:** Based on top app revenues and user base, the pure alarm app market (subscription + ad revenue) is estimated at **$300M-$500M globally** as of 2025. This is derived from:
- Top 10 alarm/sleep apps generating ~$150M combined annual revenue
- Long tail of hundreds of smaller apps
- Ad revenue not captured in app store figures

## 7. Key Takeaways for Product Strategy

1. **The market is real and growing:** $1B-$3B for sleep apps, 14-18% CAGR. Not a niche.

2. **Alarmy proves the model:** A single alarm app doing $15M+/year with 82M downloads. No VC needed to build — they raised only $15M total and are already profitable from ads + subs.

3. **Sleep Cycle proves subscription works but is struggling:** $24M revenue, 26.6% margins, but losing subscribers. There is an opening for a better product.

4. **The gap is post-wake behavior:** Alarmy forces you awake (missions to dismiss). Sleep Cycle tracks your sleep. ShutEye does sounds. **Nobody owns the 5-30 minutes after the alarm goes off.** That is the "decision void" opportunity.

5. **Conversion rates are low but ARPU is decent:** 3% free-to-paid is typical, but $2-9/month ARPU means even modest conversion on a large base = significant revenue.

6. **The user base is massive:** 2.7B+ people use their phone as an alarm. Even capturing 0.1% paying users at $50/year = $135M ARR.

7. **80% of revenue comes from subscriptions** in H&F category — validates the subscription model.

8. **Android dominates (64%)** — must be cross-platform from day 1.
