## Core Values

These drive every product decision, marketing message, and support interaction:

### 1. Enforcement Over Encouragement
We don't gently suggest. We enforce. The user chose discipline — we hold them to it. Every feature must have teeth. If a user can ignore it, it's not working.

### 2. Personality Over Polish
A perfect app with no soul loses to an imperfect app with character. The Dare Engine is the product. Copy, tone, and attitude matter more than pixel-perfect animations. We'd rather ship a screen with great copy and basic UI than beautiful UI with generic copy.

### 3. Score Over Feelings
We measure everything. Life Score is the truth — it doesn't care about excuses. This objectivity is what users trust and what makes the app addictive. If we can't measure it, we don't ship it.

### 4. Retention Over Acquisition
We'd rather have 100K users who open the app every day for a year than 10M downloads with 5% D30 retention. Every feature is judged by: does this make someone come back tomorrow?

### 5. Global From Day One
No India-first or US-first thinking. English as launch language, but every decision (naming, copy, pricing, imagery) must work for someone in Tokyo, Sao Paulo, Berlin, and Lagos.

## Problem Statement

### The Problem
**69-70% of people live with social jet lag — a mismatch between when their body wants to wake and when life demands it. The result: they can't wake up at their intended time, and the 5-30 minutes after waking is a decision void where every good intention dies.**

- 57% of adults hit snooze daily (Mass General Brigham, 21K participants)
- 82% of people who try to become "morning people" quit within 21 days
- 69-70% of people have >1 hour of social jet lag (Roenneberg)
- 2 hours of social jet lag doubles diabetes risk
- Late chronotypes have up to 26.2% daily performance variation — scheduling matters enormously for them

### Why It's Unsolved

**Alarm apps stop too early.** Alarmy forces you awake with missions — then abandons you. The moment you dismiss the alarm, you're on your own. Zero chronotype awareness.

**Habit apps start too late.** Fabulous, Habitica, and Streaks assume you're already awake, motivated, and ready. They don't account for sleep inertia (15-70 minutes of impaired decision-making after waking).

**Wellness apps are too passive.** Calm, Headspace, and Sleep Cycle track and suggest. They never enforce. "You slept poorly last night" is information, not intervention.

**Sleep trackers detect but don't act.** Oura detects your chronotype (takes 90 days, needs $300 hardware) but never intervenes. RISE predicts energy curves but doesn't shift your schedule. No app combines detection + shifting + enforcement.

**Nobody respects biology.** Every alarm and morning routine app assumes everyone should be a 5 AM person. But chronotype is ~50% genetic (351 gene variants, 697K participant GWAS). A night owl's "good morning" might be 8:30 AM — and that's biologically correct.

**Nobody owns the full cycle.** Sleep discipline (night) → alarm enforcement (wake) → morning routine (morning) → daily follow-through (day) are treated as four separate problems by four separate app categories. They're one problem.

### The Insight
> "The gap between waking up and being productive is where every good intention dies. But the deeper problem is that most people are fighting their own biology. An app that understands your chronotype, helps you work WITH it, and enforces the schedule YOU chose — that's the missing piece."

## North Star Metric

**Daily Active Streakers (DAS):** Number of users who completed their morning routine (alarm dismissed + at least one morning action) on a given day.

This metric captures:
- Retention (they came back today)
- Engagement (they didn't just open the app, they completed the routine)
- Value delivery (the product is working for them)
- Revenue correlation (active streakers are 10x more likely to renew)

## Strategic Moats (Defensibility Over Time)

### Year 1: Personality + Science Moat
The Dare Engine tone is hard to copy. Chronotype-aware enforcement backed by real neuroscience (Burgess shifting protocol, Roenneberg social jet lag, Lally habit formation) sets us apart from every alarm app that treats users as one-size-fits-all. Competitors can copy features but not voice + science combined.

### Year 2: Data Moat
Months of sleep/wake/routine data per user enables passive chronotype detection (89% accuracy from phone data alone), personalized shifting protocols, and predictions no new entrant can match. Your chronotype profile, social jet lag reduction, and Life Score history create switching costs.

### Year 3: Network Moat
Group challenges, friend accountability, leaderboards create switching costs. Your streak history, your score, your friends are all here.

### Year 4+: Category Moat
If we define "chronotype-aware behavior enforcement" as a category, we become the default. Like Duolingo is to language learning — not the only option, but the first one anyone thinks of.
