## SUMMARY STATS

- **Total unique quotes collected:** 85+
- **Sources:** App Store (iOS), Google Play, Product Hunt, Opal Community Forum (community.opalapp.com), personal blogs, Substack newsletters, MakeUseOf, Helios student newspaper
- **Date range:** March 2023 — April 2026
- **Sentiment breakdown:** ~35 positive, ~30 negative, ~10 pricing-specific, ~10 bypass/reliability

## KEY PATTERNS FOR SNOOZEWAR

1. **Price is the #1 complaint** — $100/year for an app blocker feels excessive. Users love the app but churn on price.
2. **Bypass vulnerability destroys trust** — Multiple Deep Focus bypasses found by users (logout, overlapping sessions, Settings toggle). Users who discover these feel betrayed.
3. **VPN architecture is a liability** — Battery drain, data usage, privacy concerns, and "won't turn off" bugs.
4. **Notification blocking is all-or-nothing** — Users desperately want to block feeds but keep DMs. Opal can't do this.
5. **Billing/refund experience is terrible** — Dark pattern allegations, hard-to-find customer service, charges after cancellation.
6. **The app genuinely works for many** — Users who stick with it report dramatic screen time drops (50-70% reduction is common).
7. **ADHD users are a significant segment** — But need stricter enforcement, not less.
8. **Android is an afterthought** — Buggy, battery-draining, feature-incomplete.
9. **"Gamification feels like bloat"** — Some users want enforcement, not gems and leaderboards.
10. **Users want wake-time integration** — Multiple mentions of morning phone use and evening blocking schedules.
