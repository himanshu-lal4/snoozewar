## KEY THEMES SUMMARY

### What Users LOVE Most (Opportunity to Match/Beat)
1. **Forced accountability** -- can't dismiss without completing task
2. **Actually works for heavy sleepers** when nothing else does
3. **Variety of missions** -- math, photo, barcode, shake, squats
4. **Life-changing impact** -- jobs saved, routines built, marriages preserved
5. **Loudness** -- impossible to ignore

### What Users HATE Most (Opportunity to Exploit)
1. **Aggressive monetization** -- features moved behind paywall, ads on snooze button, $60/yr feels expensive
2. **Reliability failures** -- alarm doesn't ring, bugs on Android, silent mode conflicts on iOS
3. **Battery drain** -- 14%+ battery from background running
4. **Privacy/data selling** -- AdMob, AppLovin integration, health data shared
5. **Photo mission inconsistency** -- too strict on angles OR too easy to fool with white surface
6. **Dated UX** -- "very 2013" design, hard to navigate
7. **No emergency stop** -- must delete app if mission can't be completed
8. **Social disruption** -- wakes roommates, partners, hotel neighbors, babies
9. **Math missions too hard** -- 4-digit multiplication while half asleep is unreasonable
10. **No post-alarm guidance** -- wakes you up but doesn't prevent going back to bed or own the morning

### Feature Gaps (SnoozeWar Opportunities)
1. Fitness/exercise missions with heart rate verification
2. Better music/sound options
3. Alarm naming/organization
4. Emergency override password
5. More movement mission sensitivity
6. Chronotype awareness (no one mentions this -- blue ocean)
7. Morning routine enforcement (Alarmy only handles the wake-up moment)
8. Score/streak system for long-term motivation
9. Social/accountability features beyond the alarm
10. Privacy-first approach (on-device processing vs. data selling)
