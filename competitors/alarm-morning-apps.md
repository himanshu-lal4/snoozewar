## 1. Market Leader: Alarmy

**Company:** DelightRoom (South Korea)
**Platforms:** iOS, Android
**Rating:** 4.8/5 (App Store, ~190K ratings iOS; 1.7M total store reviews across platforms)
**Downloads:** 75M+ users worldwide; ~500K downloads/month recently
**Revenue:** ~$500K/month (est. recent); $11.2M in 2021 (292.6% 3-year growth at that time)

### Pricing
| Tier | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | Basic missions (math, shake, photo, barcode, memory game), sleep sounds |
| Premium Monthly | ~$7/month |
| Premium Annual | ~$59.99/year |
| Free Trial | 7 days |

### Core Features
- **Wake-up missions:** Math problems, memory games, shaking phone, taking a photo of a specific location, barcode scanning, typing, walking steps, squats
- **Premium missions:** Multiple missions in sequence, typing, walking, squat missions
- **Sleep tracking:** Snoring severity, sleep cycle monitoring
- **Sleep sounds:** ASMR, rain, white noise
- **Anti-bypass:** Immune to volume-down and silent mode; alarm persists through app kills
- **Premium extras:** Extra loud effect, time pressure mode, wake-up check (re-alarm if you fall back asleep), label reminders, backup sound

### What Users Love
- Actually forces heavy sleepers out of bed -- the QR code / photo mission is universally praised
- Variety of missions keeps it fresh; users rotate missions when they adapt
- Loud alarm that cannot be silenced through normal phone controls
- "Only alarm that actually works" sentiment is extremely common
- Sleep sounds are a nice bonus

### What Users HATE (1-2 Star Reviews) -- CRITICAL
- **Ads in the snooze flow:** Popup ads hijack the snooze button; users must watch an ad to get one extra snooze. Major backlash -- covered by Futurism and tech press
- **Privacy concerns:** Policy allows selling data to AdMob, AppLovin, and other ad networks
- **Photo mission is easily fooled:** Taking a photo of any white surface (wall, sheets) can dismiss the alarm
- **Battery drain:** App runs continuously in background; users report 14%+ battery drain overnight
- **Can't stop accidental alarms:** Alarm sometimes re-triggers hours later; no easy emergency shutoff
- **Aggressive monetization:** Premium paywall for many useful features; frequent upsell prompts
- **Glitchy missions:** Missions sometimes don't register completion, leaving users stuck with a blaring alarm
- **Slow in the morning:** App lag when you're half-asleep and trying to complete a mission is infuriating
- **Adaptation over time:** Heavy sleepers eventually learn to do missions while half-asleep and go back to bed

### What's MISSING (User Requests)
- Social accountability (alarm shared with a friend/partner)
- Integration with smart home (lights, coffee maker)
- Better sleep analysis and actionable advice (not just tracking)
- Gradual wake-up / sunrise simulation
- Morning routine guidance after the alarm is dismissed
- Ad-free experience at a reasonable price without the annual subscription
- Reliable photo recognition that can't be fooled

## 2. Direct Competitors (Mission/Task-Based Alarms)

### 2a. I Can't Wake Up! Alarm Clock

**Platforms:** Android, iOS
**Rating:** ~4.5/5 (Play Store)
**Pricing:** Free (ad-supported) + paid ad-free version

#### Core Features
- 8 wake-up tasks: Math, Memory, Order, Repeat, Barcode scan, Rewrite, Shake, Match
- **Awake Test:** Re-triggers alarm minutes later if you fail a follow-up test
- Smooth wake-up with gradual volume increase
- Anti-quit prevention (blocks app force-close during alarm)

#### What Users Love
- Barcode scanner forces physical movement (e.g., scan face wash in bathroom)
- Awake Test is a unique differentiator -- catches people who complete tasks half-asleep
- Simple, no-nonsense interface
- Affordable / free

#### What Users Hate
- Ads block the "are you awake?" feature at the worst possible time
- Limited sleep tracking
- UI feels dated compared to Alarmy

#### What's Missing
- Sleep tracking / sleep sounds
- Smart alarm (wake during light sleep)
- Morning routine integration

## 3. Smart/Sleep-Cycle Alarm Apps

### 3a. Sleep Cycle

**Company:** Sleep Cycle AB (publicly traded on Nasdaq Stockholm: SLEEP)
**Platforms:** iOS, Android
**Rating:** 4.7/5 (App Store)
**Downloads:** 30M+
**Revenue:** ~$25.2M trailing 12 months (as of Dec 2025); ~$1M/month recent; 2026 expected slightly below 2025 (investment year)
**Est. Monthly Downloads:** ~200K

#### Pricing
| Tier | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | Basic sleep tracking, limited smart alarm |
| Premium Annual | ~$39.99/year |
| Premium Monthly | ~$9.99/month |
| Free Trial | 30 days |

#### Core Features
- **Smart alarm:** Wakes you during lightest sleep phase within a 30-minute window
- Sleep tracking via phone microphone + accelerometer (no wearable needed)
- Detailed sleep quality analysis and trends
- Snoring detection
- Sleep notes (track factors affecting sleep)
- Sleep aid sounds

#### What Users Love
- Smart alarm genuinely makes mornings feel better
- No wearable required -- just place phone on nightstand/bed
- Clean, beautiful interface
- Long track record (since 2009)
- Scientific credibility

#### What Users HATE
- **Inaccurate tracking:** Shows user asleep when awake and vice versa; sometimes records only 1.5 hours of an 8-hour sleep
- **Contradictory data:** Shows snoring and "awake" simultaneously
- **Subscription creep:** Features that were once free now paywalled
- **Customer support:** Responses feel like AI bots; unhelpful troubleshooting
- **Cancellation difficulties:** Errors across devices; some users had to email support
- **Billing complaints:** Charges after failed promotional activations
- **Reliability decline:** Long-time users report quality has degraded over years

#### What's Missing
- Mission-based alarms for heavy sleepers
- Morning routine integration
- Smart home integration
- Social features

### 3c. Sleep as Android

**Platforms:** Android only (with Wear OS, Garmin support)
**Rating:** ~4.4/5 (Play Store)
**Pricing:** 14-day full trial free; then $4.99/month or $49.99/year; lifetime unlock available

#### Core Features
- Sleep cycle tracking with smart wake-up
- CAPTCHA wake-up tasks (math, QR, NFC, sheep counting)
- Sleep talk and snoring recording
- Anti-snoring alerts
- Integration with Samsung Health, Health Connect, Wear OS, Garmin
- Jet lag prevention
- Nature sound alarms + lullabies with binaural tones
- Sleep deficit and deep sleep statistics
- Smart light integration (Philips Hue, etc.)

#### What Users Love
- Most feature-complete sleep + alarm app on Android
- Combines sleep tracking AND mission-based alarms (rare!)
- Smart light integration
- Reasonable pricing; lifetime option exists
- Long-time users are extremely loyal

#### What Users HATE
- **Alarm screen bug:** Sometimes alarm screen doesn't appear, making it impossible to turn off without restarting phone
- **Excessive notifications:** Sleep-related notifications beep/vibrate too aggressively
- **Stop button sometimes fails:** Pressing stop doesn't always work
- **Minor bugs persist for years:** Mistranslations and UI glitches never fixed
- **No iOS version**

#### What's Missing
- iOS version
- Morning routine features
- Social accountability

## 4. Sleep Tracking Apps (with Alarm Features)

### 4a. RISE (by Rise Science)

**Platforms:** iOS, Android
**Rating:** 4.8/5 (App Store, ~76K reviews)
**Downloads:** 10M+ users
**Revenue:** Est. $60/user/year subscription
**Funding:** $15.5M total (Goodwater Capital, True Ventures, Freestyle Capital, High Alpha)
**Accolades:** Apple Best Apps of 2026

#### Pricing
| Tier | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free Trial | 7 days |
| Annual | $59.99/year |

#### Core Features
- **Sleep debt tracking:** Core differentiator -- shows cumulative sleep debt and how to pay it back
- **Circadian rhythm mapping:** Personalized daily energy schedule
- **16 science-based habits:** Timed nudges throughout the day
- Smart alarm clock
- Widgets, calendar integration
- Sleeping sounds, meditation guides
- Sleep knowledge library

#### What Users Love
- Sleep debt concept is eye-opening and motivating
- Energy schedule helps plan the day around natural peaks/dips
- Scientifically rigorous
- Beautiful design
- Actually changes behavior, not just tracks it

#### What Users HATE
- **Auto-enrollment after trial:** Automatically charges $60/year after 7-day trial
- **Billing issues:** Users charged after canceling; multiple sign-up paths create confusion
- **Doesn't work for non-standard schedules:** Glitches for shift workers or irregular sleepers
- **Inaccurate notifications:** Shows conflicting sleep debt data
- **Laggy/slow:** Performance issues, especially in morning when you need it most
- **Overwhelming content:** Learning center feels crowded; too much info crammed in
- **No affordable tier:** Users want cheaper monthly/weekly options

#### What's Missing
- Alarm missions for heavy sleepers
- Morning routine step-by-step guidance
- Social features
- Support for irregular/shift-work schedules

## 8. Key Gaps and Opportunities

### Universal Pain Points Across ALL Apps

| Pain Point | Frequency | Apps Affected |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| Predatory billing / subscription traps | Very High | Fabulous, Rise, Sleep Cycle, Alarmy |
| Poor customer support | High | Sleep Cycle, Rise, Fabulous |
| Accuracy issues with sleep tracking | High | Sleep Cycle, Pillow, Rise |
| Ads disrupting core alarm functionality | High | Alarmy, Mornify, I Can't Wake Up |
| Battery drain | Medium | Alarmy, Sleep Cycle |
| No iOS + Android parity | High | AMdroid, Sleep as Android, Pillow, AutoSleep |

### Features NO App Does Well (Biggest Opportunities)

1. **Alarm + Morning Routine in One App:** No app seamlessly connects "wake up" to "complete your morning routine." Alarmy wakes you up and then abandons you. Routinery guides your routine but has no alarm. There is a massive gap between the alarm dismiss and the first productive action.

2. **Social Accountability:** Almost zero apps offer real-time social features (e.g., "your friend is already up," paired alarms with a partner, group morning challenges). WAKEorDONATE touches financial accountability but no app does social well.

3. **Smart Home Integration:** Sleep as Android has basic Philips Hue support, but no app meaningfully controls lights, coffee makers, thermostats, or blinds as part of the wake-up flow. Users request this constantly.

4. **Shift Worker / Non-Standard Schedule Support:** Rise and Sleep Cycle break for non-9-to-5 sleepers. Shift workers, new parents, and irregular-schedule workers are underserved.

5. **Adaptive Difficulty:** Users adapt to alarm missions and sleep through them again. No app dynamically increases mission difficulty or rotates challenges based on user behavior patterns.

6. **Transparent, Fair Pricing:** The entire category has a trust problem. AutoSleep ($7.99 one-time) and Streaks ($4.99 one-time) prove users will pay upfront for quality. The subscription-with-dark-patterns approach is eroding trust across Alarmy, Fabulous, Rise, and Sleep Cycle.

7. **ADHD/Neurodivergent-Friendly Design:** Only My Morning Routine explicitly addresses this. Given that ADHD is one of the top reasons people struggle with mornings, this is a massive underserved segment.

8. **Post-Alarm Guidance:** Every alarm app stops at "you're awake." Nobody helps with the critical 5-30 minutes after waking -- hydration reminders, light exposure guidance, screen-free periods, or guided morning movement.

9. **Privacy-First Approach:** AutoSleep is the only app that explicitly markets zero tracking, zero ads, zero third-party code. In a post-Alarmy-ad-controversy world, privacy is a real differentiator.

10. **Cross-Platform with True Parity:** The best Android alarm (AMdroid/Sleep as Android) has no iOS version. The best iOS sleep tracker (AutoSleep) has no Android version. No app excels equally on both platforms.
